The Mouse Action Recognition System (MARS), is a deep learning based system that enables researchers to automatically track the pose of a black and a white mouse form different camera views ( top and front), computes interpretable features and describes the behavior of socially interactive mice for useful behavior analysis. Our system combines an user friendly graphical user interface with fast and powerful computer vision and machine learning algorithms.

The proposed software automatically computes interpretable representation of mouse pose and quantitative measures of social behavior up to two mice from untrimmed videos using computer vision and machine learning techniques. MARS automatically tracks and accurately annotates social behavior of multiple mice, allowing to progress towards understanding mechanisms underlying social interactions and their disorders such as autism and PTSD.

This system is tested on an unprecedented dataset of videos of interacting pairs of mice, which was collected as part of state-of-the-art neurophysiological study of behavior. The dataset comprises over 28 hours (3 million frames) of annotated videos recorded at high-resolution. We find that our novel representation of the parts based pose and trajectory features, used in a discriminative framework, are more informative that blob based pose. Our approach reaches a mean average recognition rate of 93%.

MARS is an open-source sofware developed by members of the Computational Vision Lab in collaboration with David Anderson Lab at Caltech.
